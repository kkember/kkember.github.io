USA Road Trip - Post #3

Ken Kember

This morning we woke up to a chlly 4 degrees in Duluth.  We are enjoying breakfast - the Fairfield Inn has a good one and even Janet is getting into oatmeal, especially when you have lots of choices for toppings....

We headed over the mall across the street at 10 am when it opened and browsed through a huge Barnes and Noble bookstore.  Their bibles section is quite a bit larger than what you would see in Ottawa!

Then we visited a very large shoe warehouse and Janet and I each got a pair.  We picked up a couple of croissants at a nearby Panera Bread, and after a quick lunch in the van we were on the road west again.

As we left Minnesota and headed into North Dakota, the countryside flattened out and it felt a lot like the Canadian prairies - big expanses of open sky and the interstate running as straight as an arrow for miles.  The speed limit is 75 mph (120 kph) - for construction zones it drops to 65 mph (105 kph)!  At that speed you make pretty good time.  We passed Fargo North Dakota and eventually reached our destination for tonight - Jamestown North Dakota.  Just before our turnoff, I saw a sign saying we had crossed the continental divide - so from here on waterways flow west to the Pacific ocean.  

We checked in to our hotel, and then headed out for a quick supper at Taco John's followed by dessert at Dairy Queen.  We have about 10 hours from here to make it to Great Falls Montana by Saturday night - so roughly 5 hours of driving Friday and Saturday.  We plan to be at the meeting in nearby Sun River on Sunday.
