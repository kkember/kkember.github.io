October 4, 2022

USA Road Trip - Post #14

Ken Kember

We left this afternoon for Yosemite - Mitch driving and me navigating our way out of the Bay Area - quite a network of highways and plenty of traffic even on an early monday afternoon.  Eventually we left built up areas behind and drove through farming country - thousands of almond trees, interspersed with canals to provide irrigation.  After heading south we turned east and headed into the mountains going through the Sonora Pass.  Apparently it is the second highest in California and it certainly felt like it!  You need your wits about you, and I would not want to drive it in the dark.

The road is basically cut into the side of the hill and climbs steadily upward.  A sign at the start suggests shutting of your air conditioning for the next five miles to reduce the strain on the engine.  We eventually arrived at Rush Creek Lodge, a beautiful spot a few miles west of Yosemite.  The lodge consists of several buildings including a restaurant, a lounge area and a pool and it is really pretty when lit up at night.

We had supper in the restaurant before heading back to our room and waiting for it to get dark so we could go check out the stars in Yosemite.

Around 8pm, we drove 30 minutes into the park to an open spot where we could see the stars.  There was quite a bit of moonlight so conditions weren't ideal, but we could still see quite a few stars.  You lay down on your back on the ground and wait for your eyes to adjust to the darkness (and hope there are no bears nearby looking for a bedtime snack).  I didn't get any photos of this as you really need a tripod to have a long exposure in order to see much of anything in the shot.

We headed back to our hotel for the night.  It is a very comfortable room, but the wall art is a bit disturbing!  I guess it fits with the mountain theme!

Tomorrow we plan to head in to Yosemite for the day.
