England 2023 - Day 6

Ken Kember

This morning we were driven to West Milton to begin our walk - there was a choice of 9.5 or 6 miles and we opted for 6!  Generally less strenuous terrain today.

It began with a long section along the edge of a field - fortunately we did not have to climb the slope.

 

About an hour's walk brought us to Mangerton Mill.  There is a nice little cafe there, so we stopped and had a second coffee and snack.

We carried on to Loders, another lovely little village with a beautiful church.  It was locked so we weren't able to go in, but a nice spot to rest. 

So many idyllic scenes along the way...

We arrived at our hotel in Bridport around 12:30.  It was built in 1798 as a school for dissenting ministers (those who refused to conform to the Church of England).  

I had a nap, and then we headed out to walk to the ocean - a town called West Bay.  About a 40 minute walk to the beach, and a spectacular cliff. 

West Bay is pretty, but quite touristy - I can imagine that it would be packed out in the summer!  There is a caravan park that you walk throught to get there - I guess you can rent a caravan (aka mobile home/trailor) for a week or two.  They are all pretty much identical - not my idea of fun when there are so many interesting places to visit here - but to each their own!
