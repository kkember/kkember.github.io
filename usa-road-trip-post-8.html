USA Road Trip - Post #8

Ken Kember

Beautiful sunrise this morning from our hotel room...

Breakfast starts at 6 and we were there when they opened!  This hotel (Country Inn by Radisson) has the best breakfast so far!  

We were off about 8:30.  We had decided to take a more scenic route through the Kootenai National Forest, but google maps kept trying to re-route us using interstate highways, so we ended up circling around Kalispell before actually getting underway. We were glad we took the National Forest route as although it added 30 minutes of driving, it was a lot more scenic (although nothing could match yesterday in Glacier National Park).

We ended up traveling through three states today (Montana, Idaho, Washington) and one time zone change so we are now three hours behind Ottawa.  I didn't realize that a narrow slice of Idaho comes up between Montana and Washington until we saw the road signs.

There are still lots of mountains, although more like the green mountains of Vermont.  In many places there were misty clouds amongst the hills, perhaps because of the colder air temperature.  We passed through several little towns in the valleys - Libby, Troy, and then into Idaho and a bigger city called Bonner's Ferry.

We stopped for lunch by this lake. 

From there, Janet drove the rest of the way to our hotel in Pasco/Tri-Cities in Washington, about mid-way between Spokane and Portland.  The landscape here is much flatter - more like the eastern parts of Montana.  It is 29 degrees here - quite a change from 4 degrees this morning in Montana.  We hope to get to the coast tomorrow!
