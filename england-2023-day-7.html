England 2023 - Day 7

Ken Kember

We headed out from Bridport around 10 am this morning and walked to the village of Walditch.  As usual, a series of fields, lanes, paths to get there.

We hiked up to a lookout area where you can see the town of West Bay and the sea beyond.  It was a beautiful clear day with lots of sunshine. We've been blessed with great weather - generally getting to a high of around 20 degrees or so - perfect weather for walking.

We stopped by three different churches including this one below located beside an apple orchard.  One of the churches had a service underway, so we sat and listened.  There were about eight parishoners there, including the organist.  It's not hard to see why the Church of England has to be supported financially by the government to survive.  However the folks there were very friendly and seemed to appreciate us visiting.

We stopped at the New Inn at Shipton Gorge for a ploughman's lunch.  Then carried on with a mid-afternoon stop for tea at a cafe, and headed on down to the coast.  We walked west along the beach for about a mile before reaching our hotel called the Seaside Boarding House. Along the way we saw a parasailor gliding back and forth along the top of the cliff. 

Our hotel is the white building on the centre atop the cliff in the photo below.

It has a lovely view of the sea, and a constant breeze blowing. We are here for two nights before taking the train back to London on Saturday.
