USA Road Trip - Post #27

Ken Kember

This morning we headed out around 9:30 to a Target store just across the street from our hotel.  I don't think we've been in a Target since they were in Canada a few years ago.  No comparison - this store is well-stocked, organized and a pleasure to shop in!  We then got on the road to head to Bloomington, about 45 minutes away to the Mouton Rouge Knittery...

I had a nap while Janet shopped - she says they have a lot of yarn, but it is a bit disorganized - a bit like the shop Janet used to work at in Orleans a few years ago.

We drove on to Joliet, which is just below Chicago to our hotel.  A short one hour 45 minute drive. 

We plan to be at Spring Creek Gospel Hall located here for the breaking of bread tomorrow.  This meeting used to be called New Lennox - Alan and Rhonda were here when they lived in Chicago.  I remember going to the meeting with them back in 1995 - I had just started at EDC and there was a work conference in Chicago.  I stayed with Alan and Rhonda for a couple of nights and we drove to the New Lennox meeting on Sunday morning.  It was a fairly lengthy drive as I recall - I think Alan and Rhonda lived north of Chicago and Joliet is south of the city.

After we checked into our hotel - we thought we would walk to Starbucks which was only 500 metres away.  It started off well on a pretty residential street, but we ended up foiled by the USA car culture!  The sidewalks ran out, and no place to cross a four lane highway - so we walked back to the hotel and drove!

For supper we decided on Olive Garden - a short 15 minute drive away...

Really delicious meal - quite full tonight.  

Tomorrow afternoon we plan to drive to Port Huron, and then Monday, cross the border into Sarnia and head for home!
