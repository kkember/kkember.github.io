USA Road Trip - Post #11

Ken Kember

This morning we walked down the street from our hotel to a cafe that opened at 6:30 am to get coffee and a muffin. It was just getting light, and the main street looked very pretty.

The coffee shop is an eclectic space filled with books, knicknacks, paintings and kayaks!  It is a shared space with a workshop where kayaks and other things are made in the back, with the cafe area in the front.

Janet was keen to check out a fabric shop, but as it didn't open until 10 and we wanted to get to San Francisco before supper, we decided to head out.

We drove south on highway 101 until we reached Humbolt Redwoods State Park.  There is a road through the park that runs parallel to the 101 called Avenue of the Giants.  We took this road, and stopped and did a 2 mile hike through a grove of redwoods - these trees are hundreds of years old, and some reach heights of 360 feet - the tallest living things on the planet.  The only way to get a sense of their size is to have someone in the picture for perspective.  Apparently these trees were slated to be logged early in the 1900s to make shingles and stakes for growing grapes, and a group of people banded together to buy up tracks of redwoods to prevent this. They considered that chopping these giant trees down for such utiltarian items was the equivalent of chopping up a grandfather clock to make kindling wood.  Eventually these areas were turned into state parks and the trees preserved.  

We ate our lunch at the visitors center there and then drove on.  As we got nearer to San Francisco, the hills were yellow/brown shades with green spots of trees. 

We crossed the Richmond San Rafael Bridge to get over to the Oakland side of the bay and headed into Walnut Creek where Mitch lives.  Lots of traffic and Janet drove while I navigated.

You can see San Francisco in the distance above the fog in the photo below, taken from the bridge.

We arrived at Mitch's place around 4:45 pm - so nice to see him again!

Mitch gave us a quick tour of the condo grounds - a beautiful spot.

Very happy to be safely here - we covered a total of 5905 kilometres to get here from Ottawa!  Now we plan to enjoy a couple of quiet days with no driving!
