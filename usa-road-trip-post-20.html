USA Road Trip - Post #20

Ken Kember

We were up early and on the road shortly after 7am as we had a long drive ahead - 8.5 to 9 hours to get to Denver.  It was pretty spectacular scenery all the way - starting with the drive north to Moab.  Huge red rock formations rising out of the ground of varying shapes.

We crossed from Arizona into Utah, and then eventually into Colorado.  After a very flat section, we ended up following the Colorado River for much of the way to Denver along Interstate 70.  Alan had warned us that I-70 can be very congested on a Sunday afternoon as people from Denver return from the mountains back to the city.  And indeed, there were a number of slowdowns, but overall it ended up being ok.  It seemed like we were driving through mountain passes for the last three hours or so - either up steep grades or down equally steep slopes with frequent warnings to trucks to use low gear, and check their brakes, and places for trucks to cool their brakes and to put chains on and off in the winter.

It was nice to see some fall colours too - yellow leaves against the green slopes and grey rock above.  Colorado is a beautiful state!

We arrived at our airbnb in Denver around 5:30.  It is very comfortable and is centrally located, so we are looking forward to spending a couple of days here!
