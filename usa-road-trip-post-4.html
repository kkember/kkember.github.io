USA Road Trip - Post #4

Ken Kember

This morning after breakfast at the hotel, we headed to TJ Maxx - a store sort of like Winners in Canada, but nicer.  Janet found some pairs of shoes that she really liked along with a few other things.  Then we headed west once again on the I-94.  We were passing through Bismarck, North Dakota and I noticed there was a Hobby Lobby there so we stopped.  Janet did some shopping there and at Joanne's Fabrics, and I had a lovely nap in the van.  After some lunch, we headed back on the road.  The landscape became more hilly, and then we came to Theodore Roosevelt National Park and decided to stop and check it out.  It is unlike anything I've ever seen - a sort of badlands with rocky outcropings and gullies running through it.

There is a scenic drive you can take through the park, so we paid the entrance fee and drove in.  It was well-worth it - spectacular scenery!  

We came across some bison - one of which was on the road - they are huge animals!

We drove up to Buck's Peak in the park - it is a short climb to the top to be able to see for miles.

Driving back to the highway we passed some horseback riders...

Then we drove on and crossed the state line into Montana.  The speed limit here is 80 mph (130 kph)!  I ended up driving 120-125 - it just feels too weird to be driving 130!  We got to our spot for the night - Glendive Montana - a small town where we will leave the I-94 tomorrow and head northwest to Great Falls Montana.
