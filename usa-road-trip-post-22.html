USA Road Trip - Post #22

Ken Kember

Another quiet day in Denver.  A few facts about this city... it is called the "Mile-High City" because its elevation is 5200 feet or one mile.  The population is around 3.5 million.  The area gets 300 days of sunshine a year!  And it snows in winter, but unlike Ottawa it doesn't usually last long on the ground.

I got the van washed this morning, trying to get some of the bugs off the grill and windshield.  This afternoon we went to the Park Meadows Mall about 20 minutes away.  It is a huge mall with Nordstroms, JC Penny, LL Bean, and many, many more.  

After we got back, I took a walk to a park nearby - the James R. Bible park - apparently named after a Denver Parks employee from many years ago.  

On the way there you can see the mountains off to the west.

The park is a pretty spot, with lots of wide open space and beautiful trees.  It connects to the Highline Canal Trail, which winds through the whole city.

I picked up a quiche for supper from Safeway nearby.  Tomorrow we head north to Fort Collins, about an hour north of Denver to visit the Loopy Ewe Yarn Shop and then head east to Kearney, Nebraska.
