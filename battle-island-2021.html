Battle Island 2021

Ken Kember

In July of 2021 we spent a few days on Battle Island with family and friends.  Amidst all the pandemic restrictions it was wonderful to be outdoors and spending time together in a beautiful spot.

Amy and I left Ottawa on Sunday morning and made it to Sault Ste Marie by 6:30pm.  The next day we drove along the north shore of Lake Superior, enjoying the magnificant views.

We arrived at Rossport, a tiny village about two hours east of Thunder Bay.  Our accommodations at the Willows Bed and Breakfast were comfortable and cozy.  

Eric gave us a ride out to Battle Island - it's about 20 minutes.  From the boat house on the island there is a winding path up to the lightkeeper's house on the other side of the island.

It's a remote spot with no cell signal, no running water, and no electricity.  But lots of kids, and plenty of food!  We enjoyed three days there - kayaking, bonfires on the beach, fireworks and more.  We helped install railings going down to the shore, and we repaired the stairs and walkway going up to the lighthouse.  The kids had a wonderful time - running all over, playing in the water, roasting marshmallows.  The weather was perfect - about 20 degrees each day, no rain, and beautiful sunsets.
