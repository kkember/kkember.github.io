England 2023 - Day 4

Ken Kember

This morning we packed up and headed for Waterloo Rail Station to catch our 9:20 train to Crewkerne.  It was about 2.5 hour trip to get there - a very comfortable ride.  

A taxi was waiting for us at Crewkerne to take us to Beaminster, our hotel for tonight. 

We had lunch there and then set out on today's walk, a six mile one to Gerrard's Hill.  The tour company we used provides both a map and very detailed instructions which is really helpful, otherwise it is pretty easy to get lost.   Here's a sample...

The town is pretty - filled with stone houses, climbing roses, and narrow streets.

After leaving the town, the route was a combination of country roads, laneways, and footpaths through farmer's fields.  Lots of cows and sheep, and we startled a deer in a wooded area.  

We walked through the tiny village of Stoke Abbot - it reminds me of the Cotswolds where it feels like it could be sometime in the 1800s.

The village church dates from the 12th century.

From there the route takes you up to the top of Gerrard's Hill with great views of the surrounding countryside.

The walk was six miles long and took us about 3.5 hours.  There were a few rough areas to go through...

And some narrow paths...

Overall a beautiful walk and we certainly worked up an appetite for supper.  Tomorrow there is a longer walk planned - out and back from this hotel, and then Wednesday we walk to our next hotel.  We're thankful that the heat wave has passed - walking this in 30 degree weather wouldn't be nearly as enjoyeable!
