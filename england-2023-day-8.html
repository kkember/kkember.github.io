England 2023 - Day 8

Ken Kember

This morning a taxi picked us up at 9:30 to take us to the start of our walk in Abbotsbury.  From there we hiked towards the sea with views of the Fleet Lagoon.  A sand bar cuts off this large area from the rest of the sea.  

We saw a couple of pillboxes - these were WW2 fortifications built to defend against the pending German invasion.

From there we had a very steep climb up to St. Catherine's Chapel.  It was likely built in the 14th century by the monks of the abbey in Abbotsford as a place of pilgrimage.  It was also used as a beacon for passing ships and and a light was kept burning at the top of the stair turret.  Inside there are little nooks in the wall where people have left notes.

We headed down the hill into Abbotsbury - below is what remains of the abbey....

and had lunch at the Old School Tearooms...

Some locals were playing dominos at the table next to us...

From there we climbed to the top of the ridge that follows the coast line.  There are several burial mounds there dating back 4000 years ago.

The views of the coast were amazing...and you can still see St. Catherine's Chapel!

We carried on along the ridge to the Trig point - technically a Triangulation Station.  These were fixed landmarks installed years ago to help with surveying and mapping.

From here we headed down into the village of Puncknowle and to the Crown Pub for a break.

Finally, the last leg of the trip took us back to the coast and along the South West Coast Path - England's longest national trail at 630 miles.  We were very happy to see our hotel ahead.  Today was our last and our longest walk at 11 miles. 

Tomorrow we take the train from Dorchester back to London.
