USA Road Trip - Post #9

Ken Kember

We were on the road by 8:15 this morning and headed for Portland and on to the Oregon Coast.  As soon as we crossed the Columbia River we were in Oregon.  The interstate then followed the south bank of the Columbia River for many miles.  It's a big river, with train tracks running along both sides of it, and very few towns along its banks - it seems quite desolate.

Eventually we came to Portland and then headed south-west towards the coast.  The landscape is much greener here - lots of trees, vineyards and orchards.  

As we got closer to the coast, we entered a forested area with big trees thickly lining the winding road.

We arrived in Lincoln City on the coast around 2pm.  Checked into our hotel, and walked over to a nearby Starbucks for coffee.  Since we had some time before supper I decided to get the oil changed at a Valvoline drive in place just down the road.  On the way back I stopped at the beach and watched the waves rolling in...

We decided to walk to a nearby fish and chip place for supper.  

The owner was the talkative sort - he said he works 12 hours a day and so far today has 14,000 steps for today alone.  He also told us this place has the best fish and chips in the state of Oregon.  It was excellent - thin, crispy batter and flaky fish - we had some halibut and cod.

We walked back to our hotel in the light rain, feeling very full indeed!
