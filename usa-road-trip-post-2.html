USA Road Trip - Post #2

Ken Kember

We had a leisurely breakfast in the hotel this morning because we wanted to stop by Scripture Gift and Book Shop which doesn't open until 10 am.  The current owner was surprised to learn that Janet was Margaret Frizelle's granddaughter and worked in the store during high school.  We had a nice visit with her - she and her husband really seem to enjoy running the store.  They have a nice selection of books and they managed to stay afloat during the pandemic by setting up an online store and having curbside pickup.  

Then we headed for the bridge to the USA - a very short wait at the border - the US customs lady was very friendly, and surprised to learn we were driving to California!

Soon we were across and into Michigan, and we headed west across the Upper Peninsula towards Duluth.  After driving for quite a while through what seemed a very remote area with no towns, stores or gas stations, I suddenly realized we were very nearly out of gas.  The van has a big tank - the last fill up was near Renfrew yesterday, and I had meant to fill up in the Soo this morning but forgot!  Google maps showed that fortunately a gas station was just 7 miles ahead - we landed there with our gauge showing a range of 22 km left which is too close for comfort - not sure how accurate it is and I don't want to find out!

After filling up the tank and having lunch, we drove on.  We stopped at the little lakeside town of Munising, Michigan on the south shore of Lake Superior - really pretty spot.

Further on we came to beautiful sand beaches with big rolling waves...

Even though we started later in the day, it seemed like a long day of driving.  We ended up switching up drivers a few times.   We were on two-lane highways rather than interstate highways so that makes it more interesting driving.  Similar to highway 17, they have passing lanes every so often and the speed limit is 65 mph most of the time.  Finally we pulled into Duluth around 7:30pm - the time is an hour earlier here so it's 6:30 Minnesota time.   The last time we were in Duluth was 31 years ago, just after we were married - we did the Great Circle Route around Superior - Sault Ste. Marie to Thunder Bay to Duluth and then back to the Soo along the south side of Lake Superior.  

There is a mall across from our hotel so we headed over there for a burger at Five Guys - will check out the mall tomorrow as there is a huge Barnes and Noble store :).
