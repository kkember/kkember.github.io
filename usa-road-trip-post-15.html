USA Road Trip - Post #15

Ken Kember

Yosemite - what a place!  It dates back to President Abraham Lincoln declaring Yosemite federally preserved land, followed by John Muir leading the effort in 1890 to have Yosemite Valley and the surrounding area established as a national park.  It's not hard to see why approximately 4 million visitors come here each year - the views are spectacular, no matter where you look when you are in Yosemite Valley.

We left Rush Creek Lodge around 8:30 and drove into the park

We stopped at a couple of viewpoints along the way.

The most famous viewpoint is Tunnel View, where you can see the whole valley off in the distance.

There are so many places to stop and walk for a bit and take in the view.  

Even though it was off-season, the park was still very busy, and it was difficult to find parking spots.  We eventually found a spot and stopped for lunch.

We took a two mile hike to Mirror Lake.  The path threaded through some huge boulders.

There was a warning sign at the start of the hike...

Although it's called Mirror Lake - the lake bed is completely dry right now.  This is a combination of the time of year (lowest water flow in the fall) and an ongoing drought.  You can see on the rock in the middle of the lake bed what the normal water level would have been.

A mountain face is visible from here, and some could see climbers on the rock - my eyes weren't sharp enough to capture it.

We walked over to the Village Lodge for a cold drink and then visited the lower Yosemite falls on our way back to the van.  Again, very little water flow at this time of year.

We got on the road again around 4:30 to head home.  We took a different pass through the mountains home - it wasn't quite as scary as the one we took to get here!  Home about 8:30 pm - ready for bed!
