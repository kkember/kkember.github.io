England 2023- Day 3

Ken Kember

This morning we took the tube to Bermondsey station, on the south side of the Thames.  From there it was a short walk to Bermondsey Gospel Hall.  

A very friendly group there with lots of kids.  They get lots of visitors as it is the only gospel hall in central London.  After the breaking of bread, there was a short message to the kids, and then a ministry message.

We finished around 12:30 and walked back to the tube station, and headed for Liberty London, a department store with a large fabric section which Janet wanted to scout out for when we are back in London next week.  We had lunch in the cafe there, and then checked out the store.  The store was established in 1875 and the current store built in 1924 using timbers from two 19th century three-decker battle ships - the HMS Impregnable and the HMS Hindostan.  

We headed back to our airbnb around 3pm, picking up some cinnamon buns on the way.  After a nap and cinnamon buns and coffee, we were sufficiently fortified to head out to Keningston Park.  This is a large park surrounding Kensington Palace - it was packed with people this evening enjoying cooler weather.

Queen Victoria was born and raised in Kensington Palace.  When she became Queen she moved to Buckingham Palace and monarchs since then have lived at Buckingham Palace.

We walked up a street lined with embassies, and then headed home along Holland Park Road.

Tomorrow we take the train from Waterloo Station to Crewkerne in the Dorset Coast area, to begin the walking tour.
