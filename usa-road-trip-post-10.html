USA Road Trip - Post #10

Ken Kember

Today will be another challenge to decide what photos to include!  We left Lincoln City this morning at 7:30 in misty rain and fog and drove down Highway 101 along the Oregon coast.  We weren't sure what we would be able to see with all the fog, but it turned out to be spectacular!

There are so many spots to stop and take in the view or walk down to the beach.  We had quite a few miles to cover today so we couldn't stop everywhere. 

We did pull into the Yaquina Head Lighthouse park to check out the lighthouse and then to head down the stairs to the shore below.

As we drove south, the weather gradually cleared and eventually turned sunny.

You can see a couple of surfers in the photo below - I'm sure the water is pretty chilly!

We drove through thick groves of redwoods, beautiful tall trees.  With all the rain and mist off the water, the vegetation is lush and green, with moss hanging from tree branches.

We crossed a few bridges too - some under construction or refurbishment, but others that looked to have been there a long time.

We stopped at a used book store in Gold Beach - thousands of books well-organized, and a few pieces of art, including a life-sized Elvis..

We picked up coffees and headed on south....stopping for supper in Eureka.

Our hotel for tonight is the Victorian Inn, located in a little town called Ferndale, California.  The hotel dates back to 1890, and the whole village is a historic site.  It is certainly a change from the Holiday Inn Express!

 

Tomorrow we hope to check out the town a bit more before heading to Mitch's place in Walnut Creek.  It should be about 4.5 hours of driving, so much shorter than today's 7.5 hours (which turned into nearly 10 hours with all our stops).
