USA Road Trip - Post #30

Ken Kember

For the final post of this series I thought I would share a bit of our daily routine on the trip as well as some reflections.

First, our daily routine...

Accommodation - we used hotels.com to book our hotel each night.  We would generally book the night before - since we were in off season, we didn't have any issues.  An exception to this was for Yosemite and the Grand Canyon - we booked those places a few nights ahead.  Hotels.com makes it easy to see all the hotels in a given area and the ratings.  We found their ratings were pretty accurate - anything below an 8/10 wasn't the greatest.  Mostly we stayed at Fairfield (by Marriott) and Holiday Inn Express.  The Victorian Inn in Ferndale, Rush Creek Lodge in Yosemite, Bright Angel Lodge in the Grand Canyon were all unique hotel experiences (and also more expensive!).  Hotels.com also gives you a free night for every 10 stays, so we made use of that.

Food...we stayed mostly in hotels that offered a free breakfast.  Many of them had real oatmeal, bacon and eggs, toast/bagels, juice, yogurt etc.  Before leaving the hotel I would fill up our two thermoses with tea from the breakfast area, and refill our water bottles.  For lunch we would usually stop at a rest area or some pretty spot if we could find one and have bread and cheese, along with our tea and some chocolate.  We stored cheese etc in our Yeti cooler.  It kept things quite cold - I would change the ice every day if it was warm and every other day when colder out.

This meant we didn't have to find a place to buy lunch every day, and with a large breakfast we didn't need a big lunch.  For supper we would usually eat out somewhere.  A couple of times we found a Panera Bread and had soup and a sandwich.

Driving in the van... we found that 4-5 hours of driving a day was a good amount.  We could do the occasional long day of 7-9 hours, but we tried not to do too many of those in a row. I would usually drive in the morning.  After lunch Janet would drive while I had a nap.  We used google maps on my phone for navigation and it worked well.  Occasionally we would override google's choice of route if we wanted something more scenic.   Even though it was just two of us, having the van was so much more comfortable than a car.  A long wheel base means a smoother ride, you sit up a bit higher, and we had plenty of room for stuff we bought along the way.  The Odessey also has a very large gas tank of 21 gallons.  At optimal highway driving, we could go more than 1000 km on one tank, although after our near miss in northern Michigan, we always looked to keep the gas gauge well above a quarter full!  We listened to audiobooks (Lord of the Rings narrated by Andy Serkus - we made it through books 1 and 2 and partway into book 3) for quite a bit of the way (unless it was a really scenic section like Glacier National Park).

One advantage of the interstate system are the rest areas along these roads.  They are generally well maintained and located in pretty spots along the road, and spaced out every 30-40 miles or so.

 I also brought a small Ryobi cordless vaccum to keep the van clean.  

It sounds a bit silly, but when you spending that much time in the van, climbing into a relatively clean vehicle each morning is so much more pleasant!

Some overall reflections:

  • the sheer size of North America - especially the mid-west.  When you drive for days and still are a long way from the coast, the distance sinks in a bit more
  • the variety of landscapes - we expected some parts to be flat and somewhat boring, but each part of the country has its own charm.  There were some surprises along the way - the scenery from Glacier National Park to Kalispell, the mountain pass to get into Yosemite, the drive from Kayenta to Moab, and the mountain passes coming into Denver from the west. 
  • a flexible schedule allowed for unexpected sightseeing - quite a number of times we happened upon areas that were worth exploring and so we stopped and checked them out - the Theodore Roosevelt National Park, the town of Ferndale, the Humbolt Redwoods State Park in Northern California, the town square in Washington Illinois.  I suppose if we had done more research in advance these wouldn't have been surprises.  Still, it was nice to have the flexibility to stop and enjoy these places.  
  • being able to get off the Interstate and use other roads makes for more interesting driving.  And many of these secondary highways are quite good and have a speed limit of 65 mph so you can still make good time.  We enjoyed these roads in Montana, the Oregon Coast, the trip to Yosemite, and heading north from Kayenta.
  • meeting up with family, friends and fellow-believers.  Seeing where Mitch lives in California and spending time with him, dinner with Carolyn and Steve, visiting with Joe and Penny Clark in Montana, and being with the believers in Spring Creek - these were all highlights of our trip.
