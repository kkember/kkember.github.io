USA Road Trip - Post #17

Ken Kember

Mitch made bread early this morning and we had fresh bread, avocado toast, fruit, and coffee before we said goodbye and headed out on the road once again.

We drove southeast about 6 hours, all on interstates or major highways. As we drove the landscape became more desert-like, although we did pass several fields of small trees - not sure what they are growing.  In another field we saw what looked like oranges growing.

In this photo you can see green growth on one side, and desert on the other.  We passed a number of canals bringing water to fields - clearly without irrigation not much would grow here.

We crossed a mountain pass - the Tehachapi Pass, but it was not as dramatic as the mountain passes into Yosemite.  

We arrived at our hotel in Barstow California around 4pm today and it's clear we are in th desert - here is the view from the window...

We are about two hours from Los Angeles to the southwest of us, and two hours from Las Vegas to the northeast.

We walked over to Panera Bread for supper - by then the sun was starting to set and the temperature was pleasant.  Tomorrow we hope to make it to the Grand Canyon!
