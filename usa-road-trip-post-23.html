USA Road Trip - Post #23

Ken Kember

We packed up and left the airbnb around 9:30 this morning and headed north to Fort Collins, about an hour from Denver.  Janet wanted to visit the Loopy Ewe, a large yarn store there.

I decided to visit a christian bookstore which turned out to be further away than I thought and quite underwhelming.  Actually it was a bit weird - christian books and wigs - never knew that they go together!

Anyway, it was a pretty drive to get there and by the time I got back, Janet was finished up at the Loopy Ewe (gotta love the name!).  We had lunch in the van, and then headed for Kearney, Nebraska.  We were heading north a bit and entered Wyoming, then headed east and crossed the state line into Nebraska.  Very flat country, big open skies, and interesting cloud formations...

With a further time change, we are now just an hour ahead of Ottawa.  We got to Kearney around 7:30pm and checked into our hotel.  The hotel staff person recommended a nearby restaurant called Cunningham Journal (and it is a restaurant, not a newspaper).  We headed there and had nachos and quesadillas - really good, and served up pretty quickly.  It feels like we are in the middle of nowhere, but apparently Kearney is the sandhill crane capital of the world - who knew!  

And finally - here is tonight's hotel room wall art - you'll recall that the hotel near Yosemite had a wolf staring down at us - here we have a terrified looking horse...

Janet says I must be getting desperate for blog material if I'm profiling hotel wall art :)

Tomorrow we head for Des Moines, Iowa.
