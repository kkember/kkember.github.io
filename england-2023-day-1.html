England 2023 - Day 1

Ken Kember

Our England trip has begun!  We took an Uber to the Ottawa Airport for our 7pm flight to Toronto.  A short wait there for the overnight flight to London.  Didn't feel like we got much sleep but happy to be safely in London.  Customs was a breeze - there is a separate line for UK, US, Canadians, Australians etc and then a much longer one for everyone else - I guess it's a benefit of being a former colony...

We walked through to train area of the terminal and took the Elizabeth Line subway towards town.  This is a new line that just opened last year, and the trains are air conditioned!  Very pleased that our TD Visa card now works to tap and go on the tube - no need to buy tickets or cards, and no worrying about if you have enough money on the card for the rides for the day.

Our Airbnb host was very accommodating and had our place ready by noon instead of the usual 3pm check in time.  It is in the Kensington area of London along Holland Park Avenue, only about 200 metress from the subway.  Holland Park is lined with large trees and lots of cafes, bakeries, a small Tesco's, and even a book store!  The apartment is really nice with big windows, new cupboards and bathroom and a cute balcony.

 

We stowed our bags and went out for a piece of pizza and back to airbnb for a nap - Janet slept but I ended up trying to figure out how to switch our phones to UK sim cards and plans...

We headed out to Tesco's around 6:30 and picked up some ready made meals we could heat in the microwave.

In the evening we had our coffee out on the balcony and watched the light fade.

Looking forward to sleeping horizontally tonight!
