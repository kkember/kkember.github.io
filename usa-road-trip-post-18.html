USA Road Trip - Post #18

Ken Kember

We left our hotel this morning around 9am and headed east towards Arizona.  We stopped at a rest stop for lunch as usual.  Some interesting landscapes along the way...

We drove to the south rim of the canyon and had no trouble getting into the park (I had read online that on busy weekends it can take 45 minutes to 2 hours to get into the park).  We found our hotel - the Bright Angel Lodge - which is located on the rim of the canyon.  We have a small but comfortable cabin.  Parking is a challenge - there is no designated parking for the lodge and so if you find a spot, you keep it otherwise you might need to park a mile away!  We were lucky to get a spot close by.  We headed out on the canyon rim trail.  

The view is stunning - the canyon is about 5000 feet deep.  It is really hard to grasp the scope of it - and photos certainly don't do it justice.  It is a bit alarming to see what people will do at the edge of the canyon to get a good photo - going off the path and  posing at the very edge with their back to canyon, or sitting with their legs dangling over the side.

We walked for a couple of hours and watched the sun set and the light slowly fade from the canyon walls before heading back for dinner at a restaurant near the hotel and then back to our cabin.

Notice the people in the two photos below - helps give a sense of perspective.

A bit harder to see them in this one, but look closely at the top right corner....this is Mather Point where most people come look especially if they only have a couple of hours.

Tomorrow we plan to take a shuttle bus to other viewpoints of the canyon, and also hike a short way down the Bright Angel Trail into the canyon.  We don't plan to go far as coming back up will be much harder!
