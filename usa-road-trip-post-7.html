USA Road Trip - Post #7

Ken Kember

We made an early start this morning from Great Falls and headed north towards Glacier National Park.  All along the way we could see the mountains to our left.  Eventually we left the interstate and headed west towards those mountains and the entrance to the park.  We were taking the "Going to the Sun" road which crosses through the park via Logan pass.  This road is only open in the summer and fall until early October.  It is narrow, winding, with the most incredible views imaginable.  Fortunately there are frequent areas where you can pull off the road to take pictures.  In the summer the road is so popular that you have to reserve a spot in advance for the day you want to drive it to help them manage the traffic flows.  It was busy today too, but not too bad.  Every corner brings into view a new vista - it is very hard to capture it in photos...

You wind up to the mountain pass for the first part of the drive, and at times you can see the road after the pass down in the valley far below.  The road hugs the mountain side and there is a steep drop off to the left for most of the way.  This photo gives you an idea...

We stopped part way through and had our lunch and then continued on.  Eventually you descend from the pass and follow the river bottom to the other side of the mountain range.

The river eventually empties into a large lake....

It is without doubt the most spectacular drive I've ever been on!

We drove to a town on the west side of the park called Kalispell where our hotel for the night was located.  We were blessed with beautiful weather all day - sunshine and 20 degrees.
