England 2023 - Day 11 & 12

Ken Kember

On Monday morning, Janet walked to Goldhawk Road to check out the fabric shops there.  Many of them seemed to be run by Arabic men and are filled with beautiful fabric - likely catering to the Muslim community.   We met up for coffee at the John Lewis cafe in the Westfield Mall in the afternoon.  

The mall has a giant Hamley's toy store - we remember Hamleys in downtown London back in 2008 with the kids...

Tuesday morning we headed to the Victoria and Albert Museum which is just a few subway stops away.  It was founded in 1852 and named after Queen Victoria and Prince Albert.  Like most of the British museums it is free. 

We didn't cover it all by any means - there are over 2.2 million objects displayed there.  We checked out the British section which shows period furniture and clothing from 1500 through to now.  After lunch in the cafe, we toured the photography section.

There are several galleries of scuptures...

Then we headed by tube to St. Martin's Theatre to see Mousetrap, a play by Agatha Christie.  It has been running in London since 1952.

It was sold out, as it often is, and was really well done.  The theatre is quite small, holding 550 people.

Afterwards we had supper at a nearby Italian restaurant, and then headed back, stopping at Green Park tube station for a quick look at Buckingham Palace to make sure all is in order.

Tomorrow is our last day here - Janet plans to check out the fabric stores on Berwick Street based on Rhonda's recommendation.  I might check out the British Museum - we were there with the kids back in 2008 but they were a bit too young to be interested and were mainly focused on when and where lunch would be :).
