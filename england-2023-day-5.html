England 2023 - Day 5

Ken Kember

After a full English breakfast at the hotel this morning, we struck out on our walk - 9.5 miles to go today!  Lots of variety in the terrain - fields, narrow paths, tree-lined lanes.  Fortunately almost none of the path is along roads because they are really narrow and cars drive at breakneck speeds along them.

 

We came to an old church surrounded by an even older gravyard.

Some graves were not so old - like this heartbreaking one below - note the stone teddy bear.

Leaving the church we moved on through the tiny village of Netherbury.

Lots of sheep along the way - they don't seem to mind walkers - I guess they are used to seeing a lot of them!

We made it to Mapperton House by about 1pm - 6 miles in.  This is a manor house started in 16th century and is the family seat of the 11th Earl of Sandwich.  In honour of that we had sandwiches for lunch followed by a cream tea.  We were pretty hungry after the 6 mile walk to get there.

Afterwards we toured the gardens behind the house.

Then we headed back to Beaminster - a further 3 miles, for a total of 9.5 miles or 25,000 steps today - a full day!  

So many beautiful views like this one along the way - it's hard to choose where to point the camera.  

Tomorrow we head to Bridport, a town that is closer to the Dorset Coast.  There is a choice between walking 9.5 miles or 5.5 miles - right now we are thinking 5.5 miles!
